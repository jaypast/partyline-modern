## STRATEGIC GAPS

**16. Where’s the referral program?** Loneliness is social. If Tom P. loves PartyLine, he knows three other people who’d love it too. Add: *“Give a friend 60 free minutes, get 60 free minutes.”* Viral loop. Cost of acquisition drops.

**17. No email capture.** What if someone lands, isn’t ready to call, and leaves? You’ve lost them forever. Add a simple: *“Not ready to call? Get conversation tips + companion spotlights weekly.”* Email list = retargeting = conversions.

**18. No urgency mechanics.** “847 conversations happening now” is social proof, not urgency. Consider: *“Eleanor has 2 slots open today”* on her card. Creates FOMO. Drives action.

**19. Mobile-first is good, but where’s the app?** At scale, you want people on iOS/Android with push notifications. Even if it’s a wrapper around the web experience, having an App Store listing adds legitimacy. “Download the PartyLine app” = perceived permanence.

**20. No founder story.** Why does PartyLine exist? Who built it? The loneliness epidemic is real—if there’s a personal “why” behind this, it builds trust. Consider a small “Our Story” link in the footer that tells a brief, authentic origin story.
